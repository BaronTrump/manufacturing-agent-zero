# Manufacturing Optimization Techniques

## Cycle Time Reduction
### SMED (Single-Minute Exchange of Die)
1. Separate internal and external setup
2. Convert internal to external setup
3. Streamline all setup elements
4. Standardize tooling
5. Eliminate adjustments

### Process Optimization
- **Kaizen events**: 3-5 day focused improvement workshops
- **Value stream mapping**: Identify and eliminate waste (Muda)
- **Standardized work**: Document best practices as baseline
- **Line balancing**: Takt time-based workstation design

## Quality Improvement
### Statistical Process Control (SPC)
- X-bar and R charts for variable data
- p and np charts for attribute data
- Cp/Cpk capability indices (target: ≥ 1.67 for new processes, ≥ 1.33 for existing)
- Out-of-control rules (Western Electric rules)

### Defect Reduction Strategies
- **Poka-Yoke (error-proofing)**: Design processes to prevent defects at source
- **Source inspection**: Check at the point of cause, not effect
- **Autonomation (Jidoka)**: Machine stops automatically when defect detected
- **Andon systems**: Visual alerts for quality problems

## Yield Improvement
- **First-pass yield (FPY)**: Track and improve by process step
- **Scrap reduction**: Pareto analysis of defect types
- **Rework reduction**: Root cause analysis of rework drivers
- **Six Sigma methodology**: DMAIC approach to variation reduction

## Throughput Optimization
- **Theory of Constraints (TOC)**: Identify and elevate the bottleneck
- **Kanban systems**: Pull-based production control
- **Cellular manufacturing**: Arrange machines in process sequence
- **Total Productive Maintenance (TPM)**: Maximize equipment availability
