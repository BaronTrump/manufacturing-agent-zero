# Manufacturing Processes for Automotive Parts

## Casting Processes
### Die Casting (High Pressure)
- Aluminum, magnesium, zinc alloys
- Cycle time: 30-120 seconds
- Typical parts: engine blocks, transmission housings, structural components
- Defects: porosity, cold shuts, flash

### Sand Casting
- Iron, steel, aluminum
- Low to medium volume
- Typical parts: engine blocks, manifolds, brake components
- Defects: sand inclusions, blowholes, shrinkage

### Investment Casting (Lost Wax)
- Steel, stainless steel, superalloys
- Complex geometries, tight tolerances
- Typical parts: turbocharger wheels, fuel system components

## Forging Processes
### Hot Forging
- Steel, aluminum
- Elevated temperature (> recrystallization)
- Typical parts: connecting rods, crankshafts, gears

### Cold Forging
- Steel, aluminum
- Room temperature
- Typical parts: fasteners, bolts, nuts, shafts

## Machining Processes
### CNC Milling
- 3-axis, 4-axis, 5-axis
- Typical parts: engine components, transmission parts, housings

### CNC Turning
- Single spindle, multi-spindle
- Typical parts: shafts, pulleys, bushings

### Grinding
- Surface, cylindrical, centerless
- Tight tolerances (±0.002 mm)
- Typical parts: bearing surfaces, valve seats

## Welding Processes
### MIG/TIG
- Steel, aluminum, stainless
- Typical parts: sub-assemblies, frames

### Resistance Spot Welding
- Steel sheets, galvanized
- Typical parts: body panels, brackets

## Injection Molding
### Plastic Injection
- Thermoplastics, engineering plastics
- Typical parts: interior trim, under-hood components, connectors

## Assembly Processes
### Manual Assembly
- Workstations with fixtures and torque tools
- Typical: engines, transmissions, modules

### Automated Assembly
- Robots, pick-and-place, screwdrivers
- Typical: electronics, pumps, small components
